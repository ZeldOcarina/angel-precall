Q&A

Do you take insurance?

Yes and no. We do not bill insurance. Watch the video to understand why. We offer super bills for the services you receive and many of our patients are able to get at least partial reimbursement when they submit the super bills. Additionally your insurance may help offset the cost of your lab work. Many of the labs we work with will bill your insurance for lab work that the doctor orders.

Does the doctor treat "my specific problem"?

Many people want to know if we treat a specific problem. Sometimes the only one who can answer that question is the doctor. Keep in mind that we treat the underlying cause of health problems not the symptoms. The purpose of your new patient consultation is to make sure we can help you with the specific issues you are dealing with. Our New patient coordinator will be going over this with you.

How do I know the doctor will be able to help me?

Of course there are no guarantees when it comes to health care, however, our success rate is 85% or more. The main criteria as to whether you are likely to get well are as follows

1. You want to get well. Believe it or not this is not always the case. But if you want to get well and believe you can then we are very likely to be in the 85% that get very good results.

2. You are willing to follow the doctor's recommendations. The key here is to recognize that there may be some lifestyle changes etc. If the doctor determines that there are things you are doing that are detrimental to you getting well and you will not work to change them then it could undermine your progress.

That's pretty much it. If you are willing to fit into the above two criteria then you should enjoy great success.
